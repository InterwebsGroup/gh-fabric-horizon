## #2: Weighted Blankets

Weighted blankets are a great gift for someone who's always cold AND has trouble relaxing. The gentle pressure mimics a hug, which can reduce anxiety and help with sleep. They don't generate heat on their own, but the weight and thick fabric trap body heat well.

**What to know before buying:**
- Choose based on the recipient's body weight — general rule is 10% of their weight
- Heavier isn't always better — too heavy and it's uncomfortable
- Look for glass bead filling over plastic pellets (quieter, more evenly distributed)
- Price range: $40–$80 for a quality option

**Best for:** The person who's cold and stressed, loves being on the couch, or has mentioned trouble sleeping.

**Downside as a gift:** You need to know their approximate body weight, which can be an awkward thing to guess.

## #3: Heated Blankets

If the person you're shopping for is cold because their house is cold, a heated blanket is a practical, thoughtful gift. Modern heated blankets have come a long way — adjustable heat settings, auto-shutoff, machine washable.

**What to look for:**
- Multiple heat settings (at least 3)
- Auto-shutoff timer for safety
- Machine washable — this matters more than you think
- Price range: $30–$60 for a solid pick

**Best for:** The person who keeps the thermostat at 74 and is still shivering. Or anyone who works from home and doesn't want to heat the whole house.

**Downside as a gift:** It stays on the couch. If you want something they can wear around the house and outside, an [oversized hoodie](/blogs/the-hoodie-blog/wearable-blanket-vs-oversized-hoodie) is more versatile.

## #4: Cozy Slippers

Sometimes the coldness starts at the feet. A good pair of slippers can make a real difference, especially on hardwood or tile floors. Look for memory foam insoles and a non-slip bottom — bonus points for shearling or fleece lining.

**What to look for:**
- Memory foam or supportive insole (not just padding)
- Non-slip rubber sole
- Indoor/outdoor sole if they check the mail in them (they will)
- Price range: $20–$50

**Best for:** The person who complains about cold feet, lives in a house with hard floors, or whose current slippers are falling apart.

**Downside as a gift:** You need their shoe size. If you don't have it, you're guessing — and guessing wrong with footwear is awkward.
